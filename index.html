Research & Scientific Projects

Scientific Work

Seven research projects spanning antimicrobials, nanotechnology, environmental remediation, halal innovation, and renewable energy.

Research Project — 01

Analysis of Inhibition Zone from Papaya & Basil Leaf Extracts as E. coli Growth Inhibitors

Investigated the antibacterial potential of Carica papaya and Ocimum basilicum extracts against Escherichia coli through inhibition zone analysis, exploring natural alternatives to synthetic antimicrobials.

I2ASPO — International Silver Medal
2023 · International Research Competition
Research Project — 02

Breast Milk Booster Innovation: Fenugreek, Katuk & Torbangun Leaves for Working Mothers

Developed a natural lactation-support product combining fenugreek seeds, katuk leaves, and torbangun leaves for breastfeeding working mothers through evidence-based herbal supplementation.

ISIF — International Gold Medal
2024 · International Research Competition
Research Project — 03

Education on Counterfeit Skincare Dangers with Early Detection for Jakarta Teenagers

Combined public health education with practical early detection methods for potentially harmful counterfeit cosmetic products, targeting teenagers in Jakarta.

JISF — International Silver Medal
2024 · International Research Competition
Research Project — 04

Soil Pollution Remediation Using Biochar as a Sustainable Eco-Friendly Solution

Explores biochar as an environmentally friendly material for reducing soil contamination from industrial and household waste, focusing on long-term soil health and sustainable remediation.

Independent Research Project
Ongoing
Research Project — 05

Halal Kit Innovation: Pork Oil Detection via GO/AuNPs-Modified SPCE Biosensor with IoT Readout

Developing an innovative halal authentication system using Graphene Oxide and Gold Nanoparticle-modified biosensors integrated with IoT technology for real-time contaminant detection.

National Competition — OPSI
Ongoing
Research Project — 06

Synthesis of ZnO–SiO₂ for Degradation of Lead & Organic Phenol Waste and Pb(II) Photoreduction

Investigated ZnO–SiO₂ composite materials for degrading hazardous organic pollutants and reducing heavy metal contamination, contributing to sustainable environmental remediation technologies.

JMC Scientific Research — Provincial Silver
2024 · Provincial Research Competition
Research Project — 07

Utilization of Rice Husk Waste as Raw Material for Eco-Friendly Bioenergy Briquettes

Converting rice husk agricultural waste into sustainable bioenergy briquettes to improve renewable energy utilization while reducing the environmental impact of agricultural residues.
